About Starlight Dream

Starlight Dream is a set of all-in-one SoundFonts. It's a split-bank.
Unlike DSoundFont Ultimate or Enchant!, All parts are below 1 or 2 GB, which means it's compatible with most SoundFont players*, even freely-licensed.

*Doesn't work with FluidSynth 1.x and SoundBlaster Players.

It features the most natural, realistic, even a few of synthesized sounds, unlike "Enchant!". None of 3rd party modules are used.

Suitable for: Electronic / Dance / EDM, Classical, Ambient, Funk / Pop, Jazz, Rock, Metal, Fairy Tale Themed, Cinematic, 80/90's Retro, Nursery Rhyme, etc.

Starlight Dream is not only Roland SC-8850 map patches. Instead, It's mixed with SC-8850 Map and other variations, like XG patches, PSR-S975 patches, PSR-SX900 Patches, Genos patches (Except Super Articulation Patches), etc. Me and Strix's original patches are also included (and it's Shared with DSoundFont Ultimate and Enchant!).
Mainly focus Yamaha styled articulations.

As Strix said, For CM-64 / MT-32 Support, use DSoundFont Gaming Edition.
For Roland samples usage, use HiDef. For XG Support, buy a Yamaha Synthesizer or Yamaha Keyboard with XG / XGLite.

Notice: If a least of the sample is from 3rd party modules (Like Roland SC, Yamaha MU, etc.), notify me and I'll instantly down this SoundFont and replace, re-new or remove the corresponding sample as possible.

Backstory

I created this SoundFont in 2019-8. Firstly I'm fill for GM Bank casually, such as Lithalean samples, Grand GIG, etc. Then, I was replaced Yamaha Grand Piano with Grand GIG, and thinking about how to fill the restricted / sampleless instruments. I have none of any acoustic instruments. At this time, I was thought an idea to do these on Synthesizers rather than Sound Module samples. This is called "Re-synthesize". I also added Yamaha Keyboards Patches with my inspiration. Later, SC-8850 Banks are filled, now I'm gonna do more patches like DX7, Access Virus, etc.
For sample cleanup, First, I download a Roland Manual, Then I've checked SGM's Sound Sample Source, mostly from Roland XV Series and V-Synth Series! I'm accidently heard a song that uses "Eastwest" VSTi, then I discovered Lithalean Instruments are based on those samples, along with other commercial sources. Mostly, Electric Grand are sampled, and it's rare, thus libre samples of this aren't exist, it's in a dead end. Nextly, since I think there're processed Acoustic Grand, I'm thought to use the same way, via less samples. I think there're huge of unrealistic samples or samples that infringe copyrights (commercial samples).
